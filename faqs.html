About StorX Network/SRX token

What is StorX?

In Layman's terms, StorX is a decentralized cloud storage network, Which empowers users to store their data securely on the cloud. Each file uploaded on StorX is split and encrypted into multiple fragments to autonomous storage nodes operated by individual operators across the world who rent their spare disk space to create a decentralized network of storage nodes that is used to store data. The hosts of the storage nodes are rewarded in SRX tokens for leasing their disk space to those who require it. StorX can be considered as the Uber of cloud storage that brings those who require disk space and those who want to rent their unused disk space together.

Click on this link to watch a video on how StorX works.

What is the SRX token?

SRX is the official native token/cryptocurrency of the StorX Network platform. The primary application of the token is to facilitate transactions and will be used to rent and lease decentralized cloud storage on the StorX Network.

How is StorX different from other cloud storage services such as Google Drive, Microsoft Dropbox, and AWS?

StorX is based on a decentralized architecture that is managed by the community, for the community. This means that StorX is much more cost-effective compared to a centralized cloud storage service while also being more superior in terms of features. StorX is a more secure, reliable, robust, redundant, and greener cloud storage solution.

On which Blockchain is StorX based?

StorX is powered by XinFin Blockchain Network's hybrid blockchain.

On which websites can I find StorX?

Does StorX have two-factor authentication for security?

StorX features two-factor authentication to add another layer of security to your account. Click on the "Enable 2FA tab" in settings and follow the four steps to enable 2FA.

  1. Download Authy or Google Authenticator or a similar app on your device.
  2. Scan the auto-generated code using one of the apps mentioned earlier.
  3. Use the auto-generated backup key. This is in case you lose your device.
  4. Use the backup key and 2FA code to enable two-factor authentication.

You can watch the tutorial on how to enable 2FA on StorX here.

Why does file upload seem so slow on StorX?

When you upload files on StorX Drive, what seems like a simple upload is actually a series of complex functionalities carried out in the background. The system performs a series of complicated tasks such as encryption of data, splitting of data, distribution of multiple copies of such data across nodes worldwide.

Hosting on StorX

How do I host a node on StorX?

StorX Network is driven by Storage Nodes, If you are interested in hosting StorX Storage nodes, first confirm that you meet the requirements set by the StorX team here. Next, you can register your interest on this form and the StorX team will contact you soon regarding hosting.

Is staking necessary to host a node on StorX?

Yes, staking SRX is necessary to qualify for hosting Storage Nodes on StorX. This is to ensure that bad actors refrain from hosting storage nodes and only those who are genuinely interested in the StorX Network Project participate to host storage nodes.

Is a host responsible for a user storing illegal content on their storage nodes?

In the European Union, the Directive 2000/31/EC protects hosts if they meet certain conditions set by the European Parliament and the council of the European Union.

In the United States of America, section 512 of the Digital Millenium Copyright Act protects hosts from content submitted by the user. Websites, Internet Service Providers (ISPs), and storage providers are considered secure under section 230 (c)(1) of the Communication Decency Act. It is a part of internet legislation that provides immunity from third-party content. This section has never been tried in a court of law specifically accusing websites.

If you want to learn more about the laws and consequences associated with hosting a node, we recommend that you contact your legal representative for further legal advice.

What happens to the contracts between a host and StorX Network if the price of SRX token fluctuates?

Agreements between the host and the renter are settled at the rate the host sets and the renter agrees upon at the beginning of the contract. If the price of the SRX token fluctuates significantly during the contract, the host will still receive the same amount of SRX tokens that was agreed in the contract but there is no guarantee that the price of the token remains the same when converted to fiat at the time. This scenario does not have any effect on the renter as they obtained SRX tokens and exhausted them at the beginning of the contract to lease storage before the value of the token fluctuated. As a host, you can mitigate the changes in the price of the token by adjusting your hosting prices accordingly to maintain the same margin of profit.
